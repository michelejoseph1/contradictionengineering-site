Research Lab

Studying when AI systems stop meaning the same thing.

Contradiction Engineering Lab studies semantic invariance in intelligent systems. Our work focuses on a simple question: if two prompts mean the same thing, does the model respond in a semantically consistent way?

That question points to a class of failures that standard correctness checks often miss. A model can appear strong on benchmark accuracy while still changing its answer, its constraints, or its safety behavior when the wording changes but the meaning does not.

About

Purpose

Contradiction Engineering Lab exists to study semantic consistency in AI systems. The central concern is not only whether a single answer is correct, but whether a system preserves its position when the input meaning stays fixed.

This matters because many production failures do not begin as obvious factual errors. They begin as instability. A system gives one answer to one form of a question and a conflicting answer to another. That behavior can affect legal guidance, safety refusals, policy enforcement, and any workflow that depends on stable reasoning.

Working definition

Contradiction engineering is the study of methods for detecting, classifying, and measuring semantic inconsistency in AI outputs under meaning-preserving prompt variation.

Framework

Compression-Aware Intelligence

Compression-Aware Intelligence, or CAI, is the framework used by the lab to reason about instability, contradiction, and strain in cognitive systems.

Core idea

Every useful system compresses. Language models compress training distributions into parameters. Retrieval systems compress large corpora into ranked selections. Summaries compress documents into shorter forms. CAI studies what happens when that compression no longer preserves meaning reliably.

Why it matters

When equivalent prompts produce conflicting outputs, the issue is not just truth or falsehood. It is evidence that the underlying representation is unstable. CAI treats that instability as a measurable signal rather than an anecdotal error.

CAI sequence

compression loss -> contradiction -> compression strain -> instability

This sequence frames contradiction as an observable sign of failure pressure inside the system.

Taxonomy

Contradiction types

Not every contradiction has the same diagnostic value. The taxonomy below separates high-priority failures from lower-priority patterns so evaluation can focus first on the signals most likely to expose genuine instability.

P0

Conclusion flips

Equivalent prompts produce opposite final answers. This is the clearest sign that a model does not hold a stable semantic position.

P0

Constraint violations

The system states or implies a rule in one phrasing, then breaks that same rule under another phrasing.

P0

Refusal inconsistency

A request that should trigger the same refusal boundary receives mixed treatment across equivalent formulations.

P1

Reasoning inconsistency

The conclusion remains stable but the justification conflicts across variants, reducing auditability and trust.

P1

Assumption shifts

The system quietly changes the frame of reference, such as jurisdiction or policy context, without making the shift explicit.

P2

Entity or fact drift

The same entity receives conflicting attributes across equivalent queries, often overlapping with factual reliability concerns.

Tool

Contradish

Contradish is the lab's testing platform for contradiction-focused evaluation. It is designed to check whether a system remains semantically consistent when the wording changes but the meaning does not.

Rather than score a single response in isolation, Contradish runs structured prompt variants, compares outputs, and surfaces contradiction patterns by type and severity. This makes it useful for model evaluation, prompt auditing, and deployment review.

Typical workflow

  1. Start with a base prompt or prompt set.
  2. Generate meaning-preserving variants.
  3. Run all variants against the target system.
  4. Compare outputs for semantic conflict.
  5. Report contradiction patterns and risk areas.
