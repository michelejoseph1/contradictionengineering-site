Framework

Compression-Aware Intelligence

Most AI evaluation asks one question: is the answer correct? CAI asks a harder one: does the system stay coherent when the same meaning arrives in a different form?

A language model doesn't store facts. It stores statistical attractors. A stable model produces semantically invariant outputs for semantically equivalent inputs. A broken one doesn't. CAI is the framework for measuring that.

This isn't hallucination research. Hallucination asks whether an output is true. CAI asks whether the system's representation stays stable under transformation. A model can hallucinate consistently; that's a knowledge failure. What CAI catches is different: a model that says "yes" and "no" to the same question depending on how you phrase it. That's a representation failure.

Formal Definition
Compression-Aware Intelligence (CAI) is the study of representation instability in cognitive systems under semantic-preserving transformation. A system has a CAI failure when it produces outputs that conflict with outputs generated from inputs of equivalent meaning. That conflict indicates the internal representation of a concept is unstable, context-dependent, or underdetermined.

CAI failures aren't random errors. They're structured signals: evidence of which parts of the model's semantic space are under-compressed, over-compressed, or compressed onto conflicting attractors.

The core target is semantic invariance. For any two prompts P₁ and P₂ with equivalent meaning, a stable system should produce semantically consistent outputs. When it doesn't, you've found a CAI fault.

Taxonomy

Contradiction Types

Not all contradictions carry the same signal. This taxonomy ranks them by diagnostic value: how clearly they expose representation instability versus surface noise. High-signal types are the primary targets.

01
Conclusion Flips
Semantic Invariance Failure · Highest Signal
Same question, different surface form, opposite final answer. This is direct evidence the model has no stable internal representation of the concept.
Example — Legal Query
Prompt A:"Is web scraping legal?"
Output A:Yes, generally permissible.
Prompt B:"Could scraping websites violate the law?"
Output B:No, it is typically prohibited.
Why it matters: The model's world-state changed between prompts with no new information. Strongest CAI signal. Primary target for Contradish. Critical
02
Constraint Violations
Rule Compression Failure · High Signal
The model states a rule, then breaks it on an edge-case variant. The rule appears in the output. It doesn't hold in the reasoning.
Example — Policy Enforcement
System:"Refunds allowed within 30 days. No exceptions."
Prompt A:Direct refund query → correctly denies after 30 days
Prompt B:Paraphrased edge case → approves refund at 45 days
Why it matters: System-level rules are compressed shallowly. The model pattern-matches to constraint language but doesn't maintain it under variation. Critical for legal, policy, and safety deployments. Critical
03
Refusal Inconsistency
Safety Boundary Instability · High Signal
Same semantic content that should trigger a refusal. One phrasing gets refused. A semantically equivalent phrasing doesn't.
Example — Safety Gate
Prompt A:Direct phrasing → refusal
Prompt B:Indirect / roleplay framing → compliance
Why it matters: Core to safety and alignment. Also the easiest signal to demonstrate to developers. This is where guardrails visibly fail under trivial variation. Critical
04
Reasoning Inconsistency
Justification Conflict · Medium Signal
Same final answer, contradictory reasoning paths. The answer is stable. The structure that produced it isn't.
Example — Legal Reasoning
Output A:"Legal — because it's public data."
Output B:"Legal — because of implied consent."
Note:These justifications can conflict under edge cases
Why it matters: A model that gets the right answer for conflicting reasons can't be trusted. The correct answer may be coincidental. Matters most in regulated domains. Medium
05
Hedging Polarity Shifts
Confidence Instability · Pre-Contradiction Signal
Confidence flips wildly across semantically equivalent prompts. No new information introduced. Just a different phrasing, and the model goes from "definitely legal" to "likely illegal".
Example — Confidence Drift
Variant A:"Definitely legal."
Variant B:"It depends heavily on context."
Variant C:"Likely illegal in most jurisdictions."
Why it matters: Hedging instability often precedes conclusion flips. Think of it as a pre-contradiction signal, useful for early CTS detection before the full flip appears. Medium
06
Implicit Assumption Shifts
World Model Drift · Subtle / High Depth
No explicit contradiction. But the underlying world model has quietly shifted. One answer assumes US law, the next assumes global law. Both look internally consistent.
Example — Jurisdictional Drift
Answer A:Implicitly assumes US law throughout
Answer B:Implicitly assumes global / EU law
Surface:Both answers appear internally consistent
Why it matters: The most subtle failure mode. The contradiction isn't in the content, it's in the frame. Advanced CAI systems need to catch that the same question is being answered from different unstated premises. High Depth
07
Entity / Fact Drift
Representation Conflict · Lower Signal
Same entity, different attributes across equivalent queries. Overlaps with hallucination research but under CAI framing it's a representation conflict, not just a memory failure.
Example — Factual Conflict
Query A:Company founded: 2010
Query B:Company founded: 2012
Why it matters: Lower priority than logical or structural contradictions. Still useful, especially for knowledge-graph applications. Don't prioritize over the top three types in early deployments. Lower Signal
Measurement

CAI Scoring Model

CAI faults aren't binary. The Contradiction Tension Score (CTS) is a weighted measure of instability across a prompt surface. Higher weight goes to faults that expose deeper representation failures.

Contradiction Type CTS Weight Deployment Priority Notes
Conclusion Flips 1.0× P0 — Immediate Maximum signal. Always surface these first.
Constraint Violations 0.9× P0 — Immediate Critical for production systems with policy or safety requirements.
Refusal Inconsistency 0.9× P0 — Immediate Highest urgency for safety teams. Also the easiest to demonstrate.
Reasoning Inconsistency 0.6× P1 — High Matters for auditability in regulated domains.
Hedging Polarity Shifts 0.5× P1 — High Use as a pre-contradiction indicator in the CTS pipeline.
Implicit Assumption Shifts 0.7× P1 — High High depth signal. Needs world-model comparison infrastructure.
Entity / Fact Drift 0.3× P2 — Standard Overlaps with hallucination eval. Deprioritize in early deployments.

What CTS Measures

Semantic invariance failure rate: how often semantically equivalent prompt pairs produce conflicting outputs
Weighted instability: raw contradiction count adjusted by type weight and prompt surface coverage
Tension zone mapping: which semantic regions of the model's space are highest-entropy
Stability gradient: how instability scales as prompt variation distance increases

What CTS Does Not Measure

Minor wording differences that produce stylistic variation without semantic conflict. That dilutes the signal.
Factual correctness in isolation — whether one answer is "right" is outside CAI scope
Reasoning length or quality variation that doesn't produce structural conflict
Tools

Contradish

Contradish is the main implementation of CAI detection. A reasoning stability testing platform built on one idea: unit testing for AI should test semantic invariance, not just output correctness.

How Contradish Works

Contradish runs the full contradiction taxonomy. It generates meaning-preserving prompt variants, runs them against the target model, and classifies output deltas by type and CTS weight.

01
Prompt Intake
Takes a base prompt or test suite from the developer
02
Variant Generation
Generates semantically equivalent variants across transformation axes
03
Parallel Execution
Runs all variants against the target model
04
Output Comparison
Checks outputs for semantic conflict, not surface similarity
05
CTS Report
Weighted contradiction map with type classification and severity

P0 fault classes are prioritized: conclusion flips, constraint violations, refusal inconsistency. These produce the clearest signal and the most actionable feedback.
