Mission

Contradiction Engineering studies what happens when systems or people are forced to hold competing beliefs, incentives, or identities at the same time. Many failures do not begin with confusion. They begin with unresolved pressure.

Using the principles of Compression-Aware Intelligence, we examine how contradictions form, where representational strain accumulates, and why coherence breaks. Instead of treating hallucinations and contradictions as isolated mistakes, we treat them as measurable signals of instability inside a cognitive system.

The lab exists to help individuals, teams, and intelligent systems develop the capacity to hold complexity without collapse. That capacity underlies clarity, resilience, creativity, and real intelligence.

Why This Matters

Compression-Aware Intelligence is a framework for understanding hallucinations and contradictions as signals of representation strain. Instead of asking only whether an output is correct, it asks whether a system remains coherent when meaning is preserved but wording, context, or pressure changes.

Contradish

Contradish is a reasoning stability testing platform for AI systems. Large language models can produce different conclusions when the same question is phrased in slightly different ways. Contradish detects those failures by generating meaning-preserving prompt variations, running them through the model, and comparing the resulting outputs.

It identifies conclusion flips, contradictions, and instability across prompt variants. Rather than testing only whether an answer appears correct once, Contradish evaluates whether the underlying reasoning remains stable under semantic variation.

In that sense, Contradish functions like unit testing for AI reasoning. It helps developers measure reliability, surface hidden failures, and understand where a model's coherence breaks under small changes in input.
